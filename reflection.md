This project taught me how much small UX decisions matter when a chatbot moves from localhost to a real deployment. I started with a simple FastAPI backend and a Vite React frontend, but the moment I pushed it to Render and Vercel, I saw gaps that never show up in local testing. The biggest shift was learning to think in production constraints: environment variables, CORS, asset paths, and the way mobile screens compress everything. I also learned that visual polish is not a final step; it is part of the core experience, especially for a conversational interface where trust and clarity matter as much as raw functionality.

The most valuable part was researching and iterating on the persona experience. Each persona needed to feel distinct. I looked into Youtube videos and podcasts, generated their transcripts, studies their linkedin posts and x tweets to better understand their flow of language and their tone. All three of them had different personalities overall. 

Overall, this project improved my confidence in shipping end to end. I can now set up a backend, connect it to a frontend, deploy each part separately, and debug issues that only appear after deployment.If I had more time, I would add structured logging, basic analytics, and a clean onboarding flow to explain the persona options to new users. Most importantly, I now appreciate that a product like this is a blend of engineering, design, and communication, and that each piece needs deliberate attention to create a smooth user experience.
